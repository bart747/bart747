Sign up and Sign in forms can be confusing. Aren't they?

Lets assume that you want to log in but what you see is a sing up page. In the “after” example, you can switch to the right form easily. The appropriate one will appear without the page reload. [The first one uses link (inactive here) to other page.]

Input labels won't confuse you — there's a significant difference between “your password” and “create a password”. Input text is more readable because of bigger font size and more whitespace.

Mobile menus with many links actually hide content.

You can't assume that a site visitor will give you enough attention and time to read everything on your page carefully. That's why you need to prioritize the key parts. If there's no immediately visible action a user would like to take, you will lose her/him.

In this example I prioritize product and log in section (in practice, it always depends on context and testing).

There are also easy to spot icons that will help users get quickly to other crucial pages.
