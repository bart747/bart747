Sign up and sign in forms can be confusing. Aren't they?

Lets assume that you want to log in but what you see is a sing up page. In the after example, you can notice a switch button easily. The appropriate form will appear without a page reload.

Labels won't confuse you. For example: there's a significant difference between your password and create password. Create Free Account is also more descriptive call to action.

Input text has great readability because of bigger font size and more whitespace.

The after example does also live validation. In Create Account, try to type in an email address with two @ symbols. Errors will be detected right after you'll switch to another input field.

Mobile menus with many links actually hide content.

If there's no immediately visible action a user would like to take, you will lose her or him. You can't expect that a site visitor will give you full attention and more time than several seconds. That's why you need to prioritize the key parts.

In this example I put product and log in sections on the top shelf. In practice, it always depends on context and testing.

There are also easy to spot icons that will help users get faster to other crucial pages.
