Making Fast Web Interfaces that Work Smoothly on Average Devices and Will Help You Sell

Hi, I'm Bartosz and I make interfaces.

My UIs are focused on what's essential. No fancy ornaments. Every element has to pay for itself by improving usability. They are packed in form of simple blocks that can be presented in any order. All that means great fine tuning for marketing goals, usability and loading speed.

Before & After Examples

Typical Signup Form:

Already a user? Sign in.

It's not bad, but if you already have an account, you need to go to other page by clicking a tiny link.

The form is the main thing here, so the “redirect” link is not visible at a glance. A user might be confused also because sign-up and sign-in forms can look similar.

After Redesign:

Now you can switch forms easily. The appropriate one will appear without the page reload.

Input labels won't confuse you — there's a significant difference between “your password” and “create a new password”. Input text is more readable because of bigger font size and more whitespace.
